# YC Knowledge Base: Pitching Strategies (Investor vs. Customer)
*Source: Michael Seibel - "Investor Pitch vs. Customer Pitch"*

## 1. Fundamental Difference
- **Customer Motivation:** Knows the problem, deeply interested in the solution for personal benefit.
- **Investor Motivation:** May not know the problem, interested only if it can build a big company.
- **Conclusion:** You will likely need two different pitches.

## 2. The Customer Pitch
- **Goal:** Build credibility, understand their problem, show personal benefit.
- **Characteristics:**
  - Uses industry jargon (builds credibility with the customer).
  - Encourages the customer to talk a lot about their problem.
  - Focuses on how the solution helps them *personally* and *specifically*.
  - Includes nitty-gritty details, screenshots, live demos.
- **Where to Use:** Sales calls, website front page, FAQs, user interviews.

## 3. The Investor Pitch
- **Goal:** Convince them you can build a big business.
- **Characteristics:**
  - **Jargon-free:** Assume they don't understand the problem. Jargon *hurts* you.
  - **Simple Language:** Avoid "marketing language" or "flowery language" (fluff).
  - **Focus on Business:**
    1. What do you do? (Simplest language possible)
    2. How far along are you? (Idea, building, launched, customers, growth)
    3. How big is the Total Addressable Market (TAM)?
    4. How do you plan to charge users?
    5. What unique insight do you have about the market/competitors?
    6. Who is on your team? (Founders, ability to build the product)
  - Focuses on business metrics and potential, not product features or demos.
- **Where to Use:** Primarily for pitch decks.
- **Example (Google):**
  - *Bad Pitch:* "Google organizes the world's information." (Too vague for an investor)
  - *Good Pitch:* "Google's a new type of search engine. You type what you want to know... we report in an ordered list websites... ranked by how other websites link to them. Those results are way more relevant." (Clear, understandable, highlights differentiation).

## 4. Common Mistakes
- Using the same pitch for both audiences.
- Putting the investor pitch on your website (unless it's a mass-market consumer product like Yelp or Uber where the customer is also a user).
- Focusing on product functionality over business potential in an investor pitch.

## 5. YC's Role
- YC teaches founders to separate these two pitches. It takes practice, and founders often try to meld them initially.

## 6. Key Takeaway
- The investor is trying to understand, "Can you build a big business?"
- The customer is trying to understand, "Does your product solve their specific problem?"
