# YC Knowledge Base: Early Users and Product Evolution
*Source: YC Library - "Finding First Users" & "Minimum Evolvable Product"*

## 1. Searching vs. Persuading
- Finding first users is a **Search Problem**, not a persuasion problem.
- Most people are not early adopters. You are looking for the "Gustafs" of the world: people with a burning issue so acute they will use a broken, duct-taped product.
- **Lesson:** Size or reputation of the startup doesn't matter to an early adopter; only the problem does.

## 2. Minimum Evolvable Product (MEP)
- You are not building the final form; you are building an "amoeba."
- An **MEP** is the simplest version of a product that can survive contact with users and respond to market pressures.
- It is freeing to know the product *will* change, so it doesn't have to be perfect at launch.

## 3. Path Dependency and Evolution
- **The Phylogenetic Tree:** Startups evolve based on the pressures applied by their earliest users.
- **Case Study (Tesla):** The Model Y has better tech and acceleration than comfort because Tesla's early adopters (Roadster buyers) prioritized performance over plushness. 
- **Risk:** The people you choose to build for in the beginning will steer the product's entire future direction.

## 4. Pricing and Feedback
- **Charge Real Money Early:** Early adopters with burning problems are rarely price-sensitive.
- **The Goal is Feedback:** An angry customer who paid $500 provides much "sharper" feedback than a free user who is just "nice."

## 5. Early-Stage Execution
- **Targeted Outreach:** Use cold emails or "knocks on doors" rather than billboards.
- **Launch Early:** Engineer a wide surface area for users to find you.
- **Anthropological Study:** Study your early users like a hidden civilization. Why did they make the "strange" choice to trust a startup?
- **Churn:** Don't fear it. Startups fight irrelevance, not headlines. If an experiment fails, no one will write about it—just iterate.

## 6. Building for the AI Era
- Consumer apps struggle because ads don't cover high AI costs and personal budgets are small.
- Many AI founders find success by targeting **Prosumers** or specialized professionals (e.g., doctors) who have high advertising value or corporate cards.

## 7. Key Mantra
- "Your earliest version only needs to do one thing: survive contact with a tiny group of people who might actually try it."
