# YC Knowledge Base: Design and Landing Pages
*Source: Zach Aniso (Dribbble) & YC Design Review*

## 1. The 5-Second Rule
- Users decide whether to stay on your site within seconds. If you can't capture attention immediately, the rest of the site's quality doesn't matter.
- **The Goal:** One headline, one byline, and one clear Call to Action (CTA) above the fold.

## 2. Core Design Principles
- **Don't Make Me Think:** (Steve Krug). Avoid jargon. If a user has to wonder "What is an Artisan?" or "What does combinatorial optimization mean?", you've lost them.
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Avoid equal-weight columns above the fold. Guide the eye to exactly what you want them to see first.
- **The Paradox of Choice:** Don't provide 5 different CTAs. One clear path is more effective than many options.

## 3. Effective Use of Animation
- **Attention vs. Distraction:** Movement draws the eye. Use it to highlight the product's value (e.g., a smart scale reordering coffee).
- **The Trap:** Fast-moving text or sideways animations are hard to digest. If the user misses the start of the loop, they lose the context.
- **Jedi Move:** Animations should tell the story of "What does this product do?" better than static text could.

## 4. Performance and Accessibility
- **Mobile-First Reality:** Sites that load heavy images/videos are penalized by Google and abandoned by users on cellular networks.
- **Contrast and Readability:** Avoid light gray text on white backgrounds or small text on busy backgrounds. "Old man eyes" are your test—if it's a strain to read, it's a failure.

## 5. Common Landing Page Mistakes
- **Hero Background Mismatch:** Using a beautiful nature photo for a cleaning service. The visual should immediately signal the industry.
- **Vague Marketing Language:** Phrases like "Digital workers" or "Activate FinOps" are fluffy. "We automatically reorder your dog food" is clear.
- **Missing the Product:** Founders often show the "vision" but forget to show a screenshot or photo of the actual product.

## 6. Key Takeaway
- Your website's front page is for **Customers**, not Investors. 
- "Simple language. Communicate very clearly: what is the problem, how do you solve it, and what is the benefit?"
