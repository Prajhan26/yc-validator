# YC Knowledge Base: Co-Founder Dynamics
*Source: Michael Seibel & Dalton Caldwell - "Rookie Mistakes" (Co-founder Focus)*

## 1. The Importance of Co-Founders
- **Essential Tool:** Co-founders are extremely helpful, especially in the pre-product-market-fit stage.
- **Superpower:** A great co-founder can be a superpower for your company.
- **Fatal Flaw:** Co-founder disputes are more likely to kill a startup than anything else.

## 2. Common Mistakes & Red Flags
- **Skill Match Over Compatibility:** Founders prioritize skill sets over deep personal compatibility and a pressure-tested relationship.
  - *Quote:* "The biggest error that I see is founders looking for someone with a skill match versus someone that they actually maybe have had a fight with before, like a friend." — Dalton Caldwell
- **Lack of Documentation:** Not writing down equity splits, vesting, or deadlock resolutions early on.
- **Conflict Avoidance:** Not taking arguments seriously; avoiding disagreements leads to blowups when pressure mounts.
- **Poor Communication:** Not talking to co-founders for extended periods (weeks/months) when working together daily.
- **Adding Co-Founders Late:** Bringing in co-founders after the idea or initial fundraising can lead to a "your company, not my company" mentality, reducing ownership and retention.
- **50-50 Deadlock:** A straight 50-50 equity split without a tie-breaker mechanism can kill companies.

## 3. Green Flags & Best Practices
- **Pressure-Tested Relationship:** Choose someone you've known for a while, with whom you've already had disagreements and resolved them.
- **Write it Down:** Document equity splits, vesting, and deadlock resolutions in writing.
- **Understand Stress Responses:** Learn how your co-founder deals with stress (attacking, retreating) to interpret their actions better.
- **Pause Arguments:** You don't always have to come to a resolution immediately; it's okay to pause a fight.
- **Equal Equity (with Tie-breaker):** Aim for close to equal equity, but the CEO should have one extra share for a tie-breaker vote in case of a 50-50 deadlock.
- **Co-founder First, Then Idea:** Figure out who your co-founder is *before* coming up with the idea or fundraising. This fosters collective ownership.
- **Invest in the Relationship:** Treat the co-founder relationship as one of the most important things, investing time and effort early to build resilience.

## 4. Dealing with Breakups
- **Inevitable Breakup:** If communication breaks down for extended periods, a breakup becomes inevitable.
- **CEO's Role:** The CEO's job shifts from repairing the relationship to separating in the most effective and least destructive way.
- **Front-Load the Pain:** The longer a bad co-founder relationship persists, the worse the breakup (litigation, cap table issues). It's better to address it head-on early.
- **No Do-Overs:** It's hard to "blow everything up and get a do-over" by starting a new company; front-load the effort to choose the right person and set up the relationship well the first time.
