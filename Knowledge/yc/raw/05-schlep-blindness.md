# YC Knowledge Base: Schlep Blindness
*Source: Paul Graham - "Schlep Blindness"*

## 1. Defining the "Schlep"
- A **Schlep** is a tedious, unpleasant task.
- **Schlep Blindness:** The unconscious tendency of the mind to ignore great startup ideas because they involve painful work (e.g., negotiating with banks, regulatory compliance, handling fraud).

## 2. The Stripe Example
- For a decade, hackers knew online payments were broken.
- Instead of fixing it, they built "recipe sites" because their unconscious mind shrank from the "schlep" of making deals with banks and handling infrastructure.

## 3. The Value of Ambition
- Ambitious ideas are **undervalued**. 
- The "scariness" of a task acts as a barrier to entry, meaning there is less competition for hard problems.

## 4. Overcoming Blindness
- **Ignorance as an Asset:** Young founders often succeed because they don't know how hard the schleps will be until they are already in them. They grow with the problems.
- **The Outsider Trick:** Ask "What problem do I wish someone else would solve for me?" rather than "What should I solve?"

## 5. Core Mantras
- "A company is defined by the schleps it will undertake."
- "Schleps should be dealt with like a cold swimming pool: just jump in."
- "If you pick an ambitious idea, you'll have less competition."
