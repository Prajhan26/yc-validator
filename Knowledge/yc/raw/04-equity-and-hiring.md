# YC Knowledge Base: The Equity Equation
*Source: Paul Graham - "The Equity Equation"*

## 1. The Core Formula
The test for trading equity ($n$) is: **1 / (1 - n)**.
- You should give up $n\%$ of the company if what you receive in exchange improves the company's average outcome by more than $1/(1-n)$.

## 2. Investment Benchmarks
- **YC Funding (7%):** $1 / (1 - 0.07) = 1.075$. You should take the deal if you believe YC can improve your average outcome by more than **7.5%**.
- **Top Tier VC (30%):** $1 / (1 - 0.30) = 1.43$. The deal is worth it if the VC improves the outcome by more than **43%**.

## 3. Hiring Math
- To calculate the equity ($n$) a new person is worth based on their impact ($i$): **n = (i - 1) / i**.
- **Example:** A "10x" developer who increases the company's outcome by 20% ($1.2$) is worth up to **16.7%** equity (at break-even).

## 4. Converting Salary to Equity
- Startups should multiply the annual salary/overhead rate by **1.5**.
- This is based on the logic that a fast-growing startup will pay next year's salary out of a valuation that should be 3x higher than today's.

## 5. Key Principles
- **Activation Energy:** The company should aim to make a "profit" on every hire (e.g., needing a 50% margin on the value a hire adds).
- **Stock over Salary:** Early employees taking lower salaries save equity that can be granted back to them, increasing their ultimate upside.
- **Always Feel Richer:** You should never trade equity unless you feel the remaining share is worth more than the previous whole.
