# YC Knowledge Base: KPIs and Prioritization
*Source: Divya Bhat (YC Visiting Group Partner) - "KPIs and Prioritization"*

## 1. Defining the Terms
- **KPI (Key Performance Indicator):** Metrics tracked to tell you if what you are doing is working.
- **Prioritization:** The order in which you tackle work. Crucially, it is the process of deciding which "important" things you *won't* do today.

## 2. Setting the Right KPIs
- **Post-Launch:** The primary KPI should almost always be **Revenue Growth**.
- **Pre-Launch:** Focus on "Weeks until launch" or "Number of conversations with users."
- **Exceptions:** Marketplaces (GMV/Signups), Hard Tech (Technical milestones/LOIs), or Enterprise (LOIs/Sales funnel leads).
- **Secondary KPIs:** Retention, churn, unit economics, and payback period. Keep this list small (3–5 metrics).
- **The Growth Bar:** 5–7% week-over-week growth is **Good**. 10% WoW is **Exceptional**.

## 3. Identifying the Bottleneck
- You must identify the single biggest problem moving your top KPI.
- **Case Study (Super Daily):** Instead of beautifying an app, they realized high-intent users churned because they lacked a specific milk brand. Adding the brand increased conversion by 50%.

## 4. "Fake Progress" vs. Real Progress
Founders often gravitate toward low-leverage tasks because they provide a sense of accomplishment.
- **Real Progress:** Talking to users, building/iterating on feedback, responding to support emails.
- **Fake Progress:** Passive fundraising (coffee meetings), attending conferences, arbitrary technical optimizations, premature scaling, and "intellectually hard" problems that users don't care about.

## 5. Mental Traps to Avoid
- **The Ego Trap:** Prioritizing metrics that "look good on LinkedIn" or make parents proud but don't drive PMF.
- **Slow Growth Deception:** Mistaking steady, slow growth for Product-Market Fit. Real PMF growth feels "fundamentally different."
- **Perfectionism/Indecision:** Most decisions don't matter. If it's a tough call, you likely can't go wrong—just pick a path and fix it later if it's wrong.
- **Downside Protection vs. Chasing Upside:** Innovation happens by taking risks on upside, not by endlessly fixing minor, non-breaking bugs.

## 6. Goal Setting Framework
- **Top-Down:** Set a future milestone (e.g., $5k MRR) and back into the weekly growth rate required.
- **Bottoms-Up:** Ask what is realistic to get done next week, then project out. 
- **The Bathroom Mirror Test:** Airbnb founders wrote their daily goals on the mirror to face the reality of their growth every day.

## 7. Key Takeaway
- "Paying customers give sharper feedback than free users ever will."
- "Run fast, but make sure you are running in the right direction."
