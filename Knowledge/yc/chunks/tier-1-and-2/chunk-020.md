# DOING THINGS THAT DON'T SCALE — Walker Williams

\#\# LECTURE 8: DOING THINGS THAT DON'T SCALE — Walker Williams

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Walker Williams (Teespring co-founder)  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* Tertiary  
\- \*\*Based on PG Essay:\*\* Paul Graham's "Do Things That Don't Scale" (Primary source)

\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS (Combined from PG essay \+ lecture)

1\. Startups take off because founders make them take off — there is no "build it and they will come"  
2\. Recruit your first users manually, one by one. The Collison brothers literally installed Stripe on people's laptops on the spot ("Collison Installation")  
3\. The numbers look tiny at first (10 users → 20 → 50\) but compound growth is deceptive — 10% weekly growth \= 142x in one year  
4\. Go overboard on user experience for your first users. Do things for them that you could never sustain at scale  
5\. Some startups should start entirely manual — the "Wizard of Oz" approach. Automate later once you've validated  
6\. Consult with your users. Founders should embed themselves in users' workflows to truly understand the problem  
7\. Focus on a deliberately narrow market first to get critical mass quickly

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals:\*\*  
\- Founders are willing to do grunt work to get early users  
\- First users are acquired through personal outreach, not ads  
\- Founders can name their first 10 users and describe their specific problems  
\- Founder has done things manually that will eventually be automated

\*\*Bad signals:\*\*  
\- Founders are waiting for the product to be "ready" before launching  
\- No willingness to do manual, unscalable work  
\- First growth strategy is "we'll run Facebook ads"  
\- Founder hasn't personally onboarded a single user

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS

\- "At YC we use the term 'Collison installation' for the technique they invented" — Paul Graham  
\- "The big danger is that founders don't understand compound growth. 10 users seems small but 10% weekly \= enormous" — Paul Graham (paraphrased)

\#\#\# EXAMPLES GIVEN

\- \*\*Stripe:\*\* Collison brothers physically installed Stripe on people's laptops — didn't wait for self-serve signup  
\- \*\*Airbnb:\*\* Founders flew to NYC, went door-to-door photographing listings and helping hosts  
\- \*\*Meraki:\*\* Hardware startup that manually assembled routers before scaling manufacturing  
\- \*\*Pebble:\*\* Hand-assembled watches in small batches  
\- \*\*Wufoo:\*\* Handwritten thank-you cards to every customer
