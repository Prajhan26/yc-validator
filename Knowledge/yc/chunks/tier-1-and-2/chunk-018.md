# GROWTH — Alex Schultz

\#\# LECTURE 6: GROWTH — Alex Schultz

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Alex Schultz (VP Growth at Facebook)  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* Tertiary  
\- \*\*Context:\*\* Stanford CS183B

\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS

1\. \*\*Retention is the single most important thing for growth.\*\* If people don't stick around, nothing else matters  
2\. You must identify your "magic moment" — the moment a user first experiences the core value. Then optimize everything to get users there faster  
3\. Virality is a consequence of product-market fit, not a hack. If people love it, they share it  
4\. Startups should obsess over cohort retention curves. Flattening \= PMF. Declining to zero \= no PMF  
5\. Don't focus on vanity metrics (signups, page views). Focus on usage metrics (DAU/MAU, retention)

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals (Traction):\*\*  
\- Cohort retention curves flatten (users stick around long-term)  
\- DAU/MAU ratio above 25% for consumer (50%+ is exceptional)  
\- Net positive revenue retention for B2B (\>100%)  
\- Growth is organic — word-of-mouth driven, not paid

\*\*Bad signals (Traction):\*\*  
\- All cohort curves decline to zero — "leaky bucket" problem  
\- Growth driven entirely by paid acquisition  
\- High signup rate but low activation/retention  
\- Focus on total registered users rather than active users

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS

\- "Retention is the single most important thing for growth" — Alex Schultz  
\- "If you look at Facebook, the magic moment was seeing your friend's face. That's what got people hooked" — Alex Schultz

\#\#\# EXAMPLES GIVEN

\- \*\*Facebook:\*\* Magic moment \= seeing friends' photos/updates within 10 days. Drove all growth optimization around getting users to 7 friends in 10 days  
\- \*\*eBay:\*\* Retention curve flattened for sellers who made their first sale  
\- \*\*Zynga:\*\* Initially grew fast but retention was terrible — unsustainable
