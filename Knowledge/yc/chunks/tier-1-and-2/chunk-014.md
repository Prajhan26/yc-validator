\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS

1\. Co-founders are the single most important early decision — a bad co-founder relationship is the \#1 early killer  
2\. Ideally you want 2-3 co-founders who have known each other for years  
3\. You want "relentlessly resourceful" people (PG's phrase) — calm under pressure, take action  
4\. Execution speed is the primary competitive advantage of startups. Momentum compounds  
5\. Growth rate is the defining metric: 5-7% week-over-week is good, 10%+ is exceptional

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals (Team):\*\*  
\- Co-founders have worked together before (ideally years of shared history)  
\- At least one founder can build the product (technical co-founder)  
\- Team moves fast — ships weekly, decisions happen same-day  
\- Founders are "tough and calm" under pressure

\*\*Bad signals (Team):\*\*  
\- Co-founders met recently (at a co-founder matching event, for example)  
\- No one on the team can build the product  
\- Team deliberates endlessly, ships monthly or quarterly  
\- Founders panic or freeze under stress

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS
