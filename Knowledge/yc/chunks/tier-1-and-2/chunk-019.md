# HOW TO BUILD PRODUCTS USERS LOVE — Kevin Hale

\#\# LECTURE 7: HOW TO BUILD PRODUCTS USERS LOVE — Kevin Hale

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Kevin Hale (YC Partner, Wufoo co-founder)  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* Primary (YC Partner)  
\- \*\*Context:\*\* Stanford CS183B

\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS

1\. The first interaction with your product is like a first date — first impressions are everything  
2\. Treat every support interaction as an opportunity to build a relationship, not as a cost center  
3\. The gap between "what you do" and "what users expect" is where disappointment (and delight) live  
4\. Great products make users feel like the company loves them back — reciprocity drives loyalty  
5\. Your product's onboarding IS your product for most new users

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals (Product Quality):\*\*  
\- Users describe the product emotionally, not functionally ("I love this" vs "it works")  
\- Users voluntarily recommend it to others without being asked  
\- Support interactions increase NPS, not decrease it  
\- Product has surprisingly high retention even with minimal features

\*\*Bad signals (Product Quality):\*\*  
\- Users describe product in purely functional terms  
\- High churn despite adding features  
\- Support is viewed as a cost center, automated away completely  
\- Onboarding is confusing, with high drop-off rates

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS

\- "There's only two things in business that make money: acquisition and retention" — Kevin Hale (paraphrasing)  
\- "The simplest way to think about it: treat your software like you're going to have to date every single person that's using it" — Kevin Hale

\#\#\# EXAMPLES GIVEN

\- \*\*Wufoo:\*\* Sent handwritten thank-you cards to customers. Built love into the product — emoticons in the admin interface. Grew to millions in revenue with a team of 10, bootstrapped, then acquired by SurveyMonkey for \~$35M  
\- \*\*Wufoo's internal culture:\*\* Team worked 4.5-day weeks, stayed small, and focused entirely on user happiness
