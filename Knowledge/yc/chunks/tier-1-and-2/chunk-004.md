# PAUL GRAHAM — "THE 18 MISTAKES THAT KILL STARTUPS"

\#\# SOURCE 4: PAUL GRAHAM — "THE 18 MISTAKES THAT KILL STARTUPS"

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Paul Graham  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* PRIMARY  
\- \*\*Date:\*\* October 2006  
\- \*\*URL:\*\* paulgraham.com/startupmistakes.html

\#\#\# META-INSIGHT

\*\*"In a sense there's just one mistake that kills startups: not making something users want."\*\* Everything on this list is a way that founders fail to make something users want.

\#\#\# THE COMPLETE 18 MISTAKES

| \# | Mistake | Why It Kills | Red Flag Signal |  
|---|---------|-------------|-----------------|  
| 1 | \*\*Single Founder\*\* | No one to brainstorm with, share burden, push through hard times | Solo founder with no relevant domain expertise |  
| 2 | \*\*Bad Location\*\* | Limits access to talent, investors, other founders | Building in isolation from startup ecosystem |  
| 3 | \*\*Marginal Niche\*\* | Playing it safe by choosing tiny market to avoid competition | "We'll own 100% of a market nobody wants" |  
| 4 | \*\*Derivative Idea\*\* | "Twitter for pets" — copying a formula without the underlying insight | "We're Uber for X" without understanding why Uber worked |  
| 5 | \*\*Obstinacy\*\* | Refusing to adapt when users are telling you something different | Founder ignores user feedback, doubles down on original vision |  
| 6 | \*\*Hiring Bad Programmers\*\* | Can't tell good from bad, so product quality degrades | Non-technical founders hiring based on credentials, not output |  
| 7 | \*\*Choosing Wrong Platform\*\* | Building on a platform that constrains or dies | Dependent on a single platform/API that could change |  
| 8 | \*\*Slowness in Launching\*\* | Perfecting in isolation, not learning from users | "We'll launch when it's ready" (months/years of building) |  
| 9 | \*\*Launching Too Early\*\* | Rare but possible — alienating users with something truly broken | Product has no core value — not even a "quantum of utility" |  
| 10 | \*\*Having No Specific User in Mind\*\* | Building for "everyone" means building for no one | Can't describe the target user specifically |  
| 11 | \*\*Raising Too Little Money\*\* | Can't reach next milestone, constant fundraising distraction | Raising "just enough" without buffer for mistakes |  
| 12 | \*\*Spending Too Much\*\* | Most common: hiring too fast. Moves to suburbs, has kids | Fancy office, large team, high burn rate pre-PMF |  
| 13 | \*\*Raising Too Much Money\*\* | Creates complacency, wrong incentives, loss of urgency | $10M raised but no PMF — expensive pivoting |  
| 14 | \*\*Poor Investor Management\*\* | Investors add value when things go well, interfere when they don't | Too many investors, losing control of decision-making |  
| 15 | \*\*Sacrificing Users to Profit\*\* | Optimizing revenue before nailing the user experience | Premature monetization that degrades the product |  
| 16 | \*\*Not Wanting to Get Hands Dirty\*\* | Avoiding sales, support, grunt work | Founder won't do unscalable things |  
| 17 | \*\*Fights Between Founders\*\* | \#1 early-stage killer. Destroys execution speed and morale | Unresolved equity splits, unclear roles, recent relationships |  
| 18 | \*\*A Half-Hearted Effort\*\* | Part-time founders, keeping the day job "just in case" | Not fully committed — startup as side project |

\#\#\# HOW TO USE THIS IN YOUR VALIDATOR

Each mistake maps directly to a \*\*scoring deduction\*\* in your rubrics:  
\- Mistakes 1, 5, 16, 17, 18 → \*\*Founder Fit\*\* deductions  
\- Mistakes 3, 4, 10 → \*\*Problem Quality\*\* deductions  
\- Mistakes 6, 7, 8, 9 → \*\*Solution Clarity\*\* deductions  
\- Mistakes 2, 3 → \*\*Market Potential\*\* deductions  
\- Mistakes 11, 12, 13, 14, 15 → \*\*Traction/Execution\*\* deductions
