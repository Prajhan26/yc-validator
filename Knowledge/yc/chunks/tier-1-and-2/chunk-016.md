# BUILDING PRODUCT, TALKING TO USERS — Adora Cheung

LECTURE 4: BUILDING PRODUCT, TALKING TO USERS — Adora Cheung

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Adora Cheung (YC Partner, Homejoy co-founder)  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* Secondary  
\- \*\*Context:\*\* Stanford CS183B \+ Startup School

\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS

1\. Talk to users BEFORE you build anything. Most founders build first, then discover nobody wants it  
2\. The best user feedback comes from open-ended conversations, not surveys with leading questions  
3\. You must define your target user narrowly — trying to serve everyone means serving no one  
4\. Track your funnel obsessively: acquisition → activation → retention → referral → revenue  
5\. Learn your industry inside out. Adora literally cleaned houses to understand the cleaning market

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals (User Validation):\*\*  
\- Founder has had 10+ deep conversations with potential users  
\- Founder can describe user workflow in specific detail (not abstract)  
\- Founder has done the job themselves or shadowed users doing it  
\- Founder can articulate specific complaints users have with current solutions

\*\*Bad signals (User Validation):\*\*  
\- Founder has only surveyed friends/family  
\- Founder describes the user in broad demographic terms ("millennials who...")  
\- Founder has never watched a user try their product  
\- Founder says "everyone needs this" without specifics

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS

\- "Get close to your users. Literally sit with them and watch them use your product" — Adora Cheung  
\- "Build the product, talk to users, exercise. That's it." — general YC advice Adora reinforces

\#\#\# EXAMPLES GIVEN

\- \*\*Homejoy:\*\* Adora personally cleaned homes to understand the customer's pain. This extreme "doing things that don't scale" gave her product insight competitors didn't have  
\- \*\*Pebble:\*\* Manually assembled watches before scaling production
