# BEFORE THE STARTUP — Paul Graham

\#\# LECTURE 3: BEFORE THE STARTUP — Paul Graham

\#\#\# SOURCE INFO  
\- \*\*Author:\*\* Paul Graham (YC co-founder)  
\- \*\*Authority Level:\*\* Primary (highest)  
\- \*\*Context:\*\* Stanford CS183B — classic PG talk

\#\#\# CORE CLAIMS

1\. Startups are counterintuitive — your instincts about what to do will often be wrong  
2\. What matters most is \*\*the people\*\*, not the idea. YC bets on founders, not ideas  
3\. You do NOT need startup expertise. What you need is \*\*expertise in your users\*\*  
4\. The way to get startup ideas is NOT to think of startup ideas — it's to solve problems you personally encounter  
5\. Starting a startup is enormously consuming. It takes over your life in a way few other things do

\#\#\# EVALUATION CRITERIA

\*\*Good signals (Founders):\*\*  
\- Deep domain expertise — they know the problem space intimately through lived experience  
\- They stumbled on the problem organically rather than brainstorming "startup ideas"  
\- They're young enough (or positioned) that failure has low downside  
\- They have an irrational commitment to solving this particular problem

\*\*Bad signals (Founders):\*\*  
\- They want to "start a startup" without a specific problem — playing startup  
\- They're trying to think of ideas by looking at what's trending  
\- They can't articulate who the user is and why they care deeply  
\- They list credentials instead of demonstrating understanding of the problem

\#\#\# QUOTABLE MOMENTS

\- "The way to come up with good startup ideas is to take a step back. Instead of trying to think of startup ideas, turn your brain into the type that has startup ideas unconsciously" — Paul Graham  
\- "Startups are counterintuitive. I can give you a list of things not to do, and your instinct will be to do them anyway" — Paul Graham  
\- "What you need to succeed in a startup is not expertise in startups. What you need is expertise in your own users" — Paul Graham  
\- "At YC we're always telling founders to go talk to their users. This is not just platitude — founders who don't talk to users always build the wrong thing" — Paul Graham

\#\#\# EXAMPLES GIVEN

\- \*\*PG's own story (Viaweb):\*\* Built online store builder because he needed one — personal experience drove the insight  
\- \*\*General pattern:\*\* YC consistently sees that the best ideas come from founders solving their own problems
