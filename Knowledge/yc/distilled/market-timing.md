# YC Distilled: Market And Timing

## Purpose

This file consolidates what the current YC knowledge dump says about market potential, market shape, and timing.

## Core Evaluation Criteria

### Strong market signals

- Market can become venture-scale.
- Market is growing or unlocking due to technology, regulation, or behavior change.
- Team starts in a small wedge they can dominate first.
- Crowded markets are acceptable when the team has a real insight into what incumbents miss.
- Initial market size can look small if there is a credible path to expansion.

### Strong timing signals

- Clear "why now" tied to a change in infrastructure, regulation, cost curves, or user behavior.
- Product would have been difficult or impossible a few years earlier.
- Market pull is increasing rather than fading.

### Weak market or timing signals

- Flat or shrinking market.
- No concrete "why now."
- Huge TAM claims with no believable acquisition path.
- Founder frames the market at a high level but cannot identify the first beachhead.
- Competitive market with no insight or advantage.

## Repeated Tests In The Dump

- Is the market growing 20%+ annually or otherwise structurally expanding?
- Why is now the right moment?
- Can the company start narrow, win, then expand?
- Is the market truly big enough, or just described as big?
- Does competition prove demand, or does it expose lack of differentiation?

## Practical Scoring Shape

### 9-10

- Clear wedge, big upside, strong why-now, and a believable path from niche to scale.

### 7-8

- Large or growing market with reasonable timing support, but not yet extraordinary.

### 5-6

- Market exists, but growth and timing are not strong enough to make the case feel urgent.

### 1-4

- Weak or generic market thesis.
- No why-now.
- Venture-scale story does not hold up.
