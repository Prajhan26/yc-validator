# YC Distilled: Traction And Validation

## Purpose

This file consolidates what the current YC knowledge dump says about traction, user validation, PMF, and the metrics that matter.

## Core Evaluation Criteria

### Strong traction signals

- Revenue is growing quickly.
- Retention is strong, especially when cohorts flatten.
- Founders know their metrics cold.
- Paying users exist, especially if they pay early despite rough edges.
- Growth comes from real usage, not vanity signals.

### Strong validation signals

- Founders have talked to users before and during building.
- Users spend money or time on current workarounds.
- Early adopters use imperfect versions because the pain is real.
- Founders launch early and learn from actual behavior.
- Unscalable early efforts produce real learning and early growth.

### Weak traction or validation signals

- No launch, no users, no meaningful metrics.
- Traction is mostly friends, surveys, social followers, or waitlist vanity.
- Founders delay launch for polish.
- Growth exists but retention is weak.
- Founders confuse compliments with validation.

## Repeated Tests In The Dump

- Do users come back?
- Are people paying?
- Is growth week-over-week, and how fast?
- Is the founder measuring real progress or fake progress?
- Has the founder put the product in front of users yet?

## Practical Scoring Shape

### 9-10

- Strong revenue or usage growth with convincing retention and real customer pull.

### 7-8

- Some paying users or clear growth trajectory with decent retention and strong founder understanding.

### 5-6

- Product is launched and learning is happening, but traction is still inconsistent.

### 1-4

- Little or no real validation.
- Metrics are weak, absent, or mostly vanity.
