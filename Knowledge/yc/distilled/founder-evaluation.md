# YC Distilled: Founder Evaluation

## Purpose

This file consolidates what the current YC knowledge dump says about how YC-like evaluators judge founders.

## Core Evaluation Criteria

### Strong founder signals

- Deep founder-market fit through lived experience or unusual domain expertise.
- At least one founder can build the product without waiting on hires.
- Founders are full-time and already making progress.
- Co-founders have history together and can disagree productively.
- Founders are relentlessly resourceful and unblock themselves.
- Founders can explain the company simply in one sentence.
- Founders talk to users directly and use what they learn.

### Medium-strength signals

- Team looks capable, but domain knowledge is still developing.
- Technical capability exists, but product is still pre-launch.
- Founders are committed, but there are gaps in traction or speed.
- Co-founder relationship seems plausible, but not yet pressure-tested.

### Weak founder signals

- No technical founder for a technical product.
- Co-founders met recently or only matched on skill, not trust.
- Part-time commitment or "will go full-time after funding."
- Founders sound like they want to start a startup more than solve this problem.
- Founders cannot explain the product clearly.
- Founders blame circumstances or other people for lack of progress.

## Founder Dimensions That Recur Across Sources

- Founder-market fit
- Technical capability
- Commitment level
- Co-founder quality
- Communication clarity
- Resourcefulness
- Speed of execution
- User obsession
- Mission authenticity

## Practical Scoring Shape

### 9-10

- Founder experienced the problem personally.
- Team can build now.
- Progress already exists.
- Clear, concise explanation.
- Strong evidence of grit and resourcefulness.

### 7-8

- Good team with real strengths.
- Full-time and serious.
- Some evidence of user contact or early product movement.
- Still missing one piece of proof, usually traction depth or exceptional insight.

### 5-6

- Decent team, but not obviously special for this problem.
- Some capability, some commitment, but not enough differentiation.

### 1-4

- Major capability or commitment gap.
- Serious co-founder or clarity issues.
- Founder profile creates doubt that this team is the right one to solve the problem.
