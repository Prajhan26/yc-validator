# YC Distilled: Problem And Idea Evaluation

## Purpose

This file consolidates what the current YC knowledge dump says about how YC-like evaluators judge startup problems and ideas.

## Core Evaluation Criteria

### Strong problem signals

- Problem is acute, frequent, and painful.
- Users are already searching for solutions or using broken workarounds.
- Founder personally experienced the problem or knows users who do.
- Problem is specific and understandable without jargon.
- The idea is not a solution in search of a problem.

### Strong idea signals

- Solution is 10x better on something users already care about.
- The idea comes from direct experience, unusual insight, or deep expertise.
- Existing competitors prove demand rather than invalidating the space.
- The idea starts with a narrow wedge and can expand.
- The opportunity includes some schlep, which lowers competition.

### Weak problem or idea signals

- Problem is vague or abstract.
- Founder starts from technology first and hunts for a use case.
- Idea is a tar pit category with hidden structural blockers.
- Users say it sounds nice, but they are not actively trying to solve it.
- The founder cannot name who has the problem or what they do now.

## Repeated Tests In The Dump

- Is this a real problem or a SISP?
- Is the problem hair-on-fire or just a nice-to-have?
- Does the founder have founder-market fit for this idea?
- Is there a real reason this approach wins against alternatives?
- Is there an unfair advantage or a real new insight?
- Is the idea narrow enough to start and broad enough to matter later?

## Practical Scoring Shape

### 9-10

- Founder has direct exposure to the problem.
- Users already show pain, workarounds, or willingness to pay.
- Solution is concrete, differentiated, and tied to real user behavior.

### 7-8

- Clear problem and credible solution.
- Evidence of real demand, but still some open questions around scale or differentiation.

### 5-6

- Problem probably exists, but urgency is moderate.
- Solution seems reasonable, but not yet compelling.

### 1-4

- Weak problem definition.
- Technology-driven or derivative framing.
- Tar pit risk or little evidence anyone urgently needs this.
