# YC Distilled: Red Flags And Deal Breakers

## Purpose

This file consolidates the main negative signals that recur across the current YC knowledge dump.

## Founder Red Flags

- No technical founder for a technical company.
- Co-founders just met or do not trust each other.
- Part-time commitment or waiting for funding before starting.
- Founder cannot explain the company simply.
- Founder seems to be "playing startup."
- Founder blames others instead of adapting.

## Problem And Idea Red Flags

- SISP: technology first, problem second.
- Tar pit idea with structural difficulty hidden behind plausible surface demand.
- Problem is vague, infrequent, or only mildly painful.
- Derivative "X for Y" framing with no real insight.

## Market Red Flags

- Huge TAM language without first customers or wedge.
- No why-now.
- Market is too small, stagnant, or badly understood.
- Competition is intense and founder has no differentiated thesis.

## Traction And Execution Red Flags

- Not launched despite long effort.
- "Traction" means friends, surveys, followers, or hypothetical interest.
- Growth without retention.
- Fake progress: conferences, fundraising theater, polish, or busywork instead of user learning.
- Raising money to avoid PMF work.

## Interview And Application Red Flags

- Long, confusing explanation of what the company does.
- One founder dominates while others add little.
- Rehearsed, defensive, or glib answers.
- No progress since applying.
- Misrepresentation or exaggeration.
